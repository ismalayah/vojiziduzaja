What Is Tony Rock’s Net Worth?

Tony Rock, the American stand-up comedian, actor, and producer, has a net worth of $4 million. He is best known for his roles on “Everybody Hates Chris” and “All of Us,” both of which aired on UPN and The CW. Additionally, he created, executive produced, and starred on “The Tony Rock Project” and has performed stand-up comedy on shows like “Def Comedy Jam” and “Comedy Central Presents.” With over 20 acting credits to his name, Rock has appeared in various films and television shows. He was nominated for a BET Comedy Award in 2005 for his supporting role in “All of Us.”

Tony Rock Early Life

Tony Rock was born on June 30, 1974, in Brooklyn, New York. He grew up in the Bedford-Stuyvesant neighborhood with eight siblings. His mother, Rosalie, worked as a teacher and social worker for the mentally handicapped, while his father, Julius, worked as a newspaper deliveryman and truck driver.

Tragically, Julius passed away in 1988 after undergoing ulcer surgery.

Tony Rock’s Acting Career

Tony Rock began his acting career in 2001 with the TV movie “Me & My Needs.” He was a correspondent on the Comedy Central series “BattleBots” and the host of the Oxygen game show “Can You Tell?” He played Dirk Black on “All of Us” from 2003 to 2007 and had a recurring role as Uncle Ryan on “Everybody Hates Chris” from 2008 to 2009. In 2012, he co-starred in “Think Like a Man,” which won a BET Award for Best Movie, and became the host of the BET series “Apollo Live.” He guest-starred on various shows and had a recurring role on “Reed Between the Lines” from 2015 to 2017. Tony also starred in several films and served as a judge on a Netflix baking competition in 2022.

Tony Rock Arrested for DUI

In February 2009, comedian and founder of the MyROCK Diabetes Foundation, Tony Rock was arrested for DUI in Los Angeles. He was initially pulled over for speeding, but after showing signs of intoxication and failing field sobriety tests, he was charged with two counts of DUI. Tony pleaded guilty and was ordered to serve 36 months of probation, pay a $1,000 fine, and attend an alcohol education program.

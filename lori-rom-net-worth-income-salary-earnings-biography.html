Lori Rom Net Worth, Income, Salary, Earnings, Biography

Lori Rom (born Loren Rom; August 16, 1975) is an American actress who was born into a family of actors.

Rom was born in the New Jersey town of Red Bank. She was a student at Carnegie Mellon University’s drama conservatory, where she also worked part-time while attending classes. After being cast in the part of Phoebe Halliwell in the television series Charmed, she decided to leave after shooting the pilot episode, and Alyssa Milano was cast in the role of Phoebe Halliwell instead. 

She has also appeared as a guest star on television shows such as Party of Five, CSI: NY, Jack and Jill, and others. Her other credits, apart from television, include a December 1998 run as Viola in Shakespeare’s Twelfth Night at The Kresge Foundation and a 2006 ad for Neosporin. The character Claire Redfield in the computer-animated film Resident Evil: Degeneration was likewise created using motion capture techniques performed by her.
